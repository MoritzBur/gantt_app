# Framing

## Thesis title
Adaptive morphing flap scheduling for gust load alleviation on a regional turboprop high-lift wing.

## Research hypothesis
Moderate trailing-edge camber re-scheduling during the gust encounter will lower transient load peaks more than a static flap setting, while preserving acceptable lift during the maneuver envelope.

## Deliverables
- Reference aircraft and flap schedule frozen
- Literature synthesis on load alleviation and reduced-order modeling
- KPI sheet for CFD, tunnel, and optimization phases

## Working assumptions
- One representative semi-span section is enough for the first thesis iteration
- Structural deflection is handled parametrically, not via a full aeroelastic loop
- Tunnel Reynolds mismatch will be corrected with trend-based comparison rather than absolute matching
